Book Summary

The Hobbit by J.R.R. Tolkien is a fantasy novel that follows the journey of Bilbo Baggins, a hobbit who reluctantly embarks on an epic adventure to help a group of dwarves reclaim their homeland from the dragon Smaug. Along the way, Bilbo discovers his own bravery and resourcefulness.

About the Author

J.R.R. Tolkien

J.R.R. Tolkien (1892-1973) was an English writer, philologist, and academic. He is best known for his works The Hobbit and The Lord of the Rings, which have captivated readers for decades with their intricate world-building and compelling storytelling.

Fun Fact

Tolkien originally wrote The Hobbit as a bedtime story for his children, but it later became one of the best-selling books of all time.
